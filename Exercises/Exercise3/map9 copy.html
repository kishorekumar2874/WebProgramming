Sun

The Sun is a massive star at the center of our solar system, composed primarily of hydrogen and helium. It is the primary source of energy for life on Earth, producing light and heat through nuclear fusion. The Sun's surface temperature is around 5,500°C (9,932°F), while its core reaches approximately 15 million degrees Celsius (27 million degrees Fahrenheit). It takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds for sunlight to travel from the Sun to Earth. The Sun has been shining for about 4.6 billion years and is expected to continue for another 5 billion years before becoming a red giant.
